Summary¶

Tuition vs Average Wage Index¶

As a basic overview, median income has gradually increased over time. There is an upward trend in tuition at a similar rate, with the highest increase being in private tuition, followed by public tuition. This follows general expectations.

Student Loan debt to Average Wage Index Ratio Over the Years¶

Student Loan debt in a 34-year (from 1973-2017) timeframe has shown a 280% increase where it reached its peak in 2010, then began to tapper off over the next few years.

American Recovery and Reinvestment Act of 2009 had a big influence in 2009 after the great recession to incentivize higher education by boosting Pell Grants and Federal Loans.

o It was nicknamed the Recovery Act, was a stimulus package enacted by the 111th U.S. Congress and signed into law by President Barack Obama in February 2009. Developed in response to the Great Recession. Stimulus was focused on Immediate Relief for Families, Modernize Federal Infrastructure, Increase Alternative Energy Production, Expand Health Care, Education, Invest in Science Research and Technology, and helping small businesses.

Enrollment and Tuition Trends for years (1973 – 2017)¶

Enrollment

Public enrollment has seen a 50.1% increase over 34 year timeframe, highest in 2010 (15.14m), has since seen a decline of 410,000 enrollees or 2.7% decline (14.73m)

Private enrollment has seen a 42.7% increase over 34 year timeframe, highest in 2011 (5.89m), has since seen a decline of 790,000 enrollees or 13.4% decline (5.1m)

Tuition

Public 4yr tuition has seen an increase of 29.1% over timeframe
Private 4yr tuition has seen an increase of 33.7% over timeframe

Student Loans

Has seen upward trends in 1980 (18,128m), 2000(), and peaked in 2010 (119,243b), has since seen a 7 year decline of 21.3% or 93,903b.
